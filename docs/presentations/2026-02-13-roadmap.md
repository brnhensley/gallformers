# Gallformers Roadmap

*Last updated: February 2026*

This is a roadmap document that was used to inform the presentation at the annual Forbes Lab Gallhunters meeting. It is a snapshot of my thinking at that time and will inform how we proceed, but it is not a definitve statement of the planned future.

## Where We Are

Gallformers has been the primary reference site for galls of North America since launching in May 2021. In early 2026 we completed a full-stack rebuild (V2) from Next.js to Phoenix/LiveView, modernizing the platform and clearing years of accumulated technical debt.

### What V2 Delivered

- Complete rewrite on Phoenix 1.8 / LiveView / Ecto / SQLite
- Taxonomy reclassification and rename tools
- Cascade delete protection with informed-delete UI
- Unified tree browser for taxonomy navigation
- Admin images table view with bulk metadata operations
- Built-in privacy-respecting analytics
- Identification keys moved to database with admin CRUD
- Taxonomy lineage domain types
- Backup infrastructure (Litestream + OpenTofu)
- Smoke tests and CI pipeline

### What Comes Next

The reference is built. The next phase is about **community** — both the biological community around galls (parasites, inquilines, associated taxa) and the human community that studies and documents them.

## Phase 1: Foundation & Quick Wins

*Make the platform safe to move fast on and unblock everything after it.*

### [PostgreSQL Migration](.mull/matters/4474-postgresql-migration.md)

SQLite has served us well but is becoming a constraint. PostgreSQL gives us parallel test execution, MVCC for concurrent admin access, richer data types for geographic expansion, and eliminates a growing list of workarounds. This is a 10-14 day effort that pays dividends on every phase that follows.

*Status: Feasibility research complete. Decision pending.*

### Quality & Velocity

- **[E2E test suite feasibility](.mull/matters/1501-e2e-test-suite-feasibility.md)** — Assess whether comprehensive end-to-end testing is now economically viable with LLM assistance. The core problem: moving fast with AI increases the risk of subtle bugs. E2E coverage (or an alternative correctness strategy) is the answer.
- **[Branch preview deploys](.mull/matters/9ca2-branch-preview-deploys.md)** — Enable deploying from a branch so admins and collaborators can test changes before they go live.
- **[Audit trail](.mull/matters/ede2-audit-trail.md)** — Full tracing of data changes: who changed what, when, including deletes. Safety net for a growing admin base.

### Quick Wins

- **[llms.txt](.mull/matters/18b5-llms-txt.md)** — Point LLM crawlers toward our API and structured data. Trivial effort, high leverage given current AI traffic patterns.
- **[Admin onboarding documentation](.mull/matters/f554-admin-onboarding-documentation.md)** — Talk to active admins, understand their pain points, write guides. Starts as docs, not code.

### [Keys](.mull/matters/85c0-keys-feature-expansion.md) (Active)

The identification key system is in active development. We're collaborating on an upcoming paper that will feature our key viewer. Current system is already strong; iterations through the paper process will refine it further.

## Phase 2: Western Hemisphere Expansion

*Horizontal growth — more geography, same data model. All work streams ship together.*

### [Geographic Data Model](.mull/matters/1db6-western-hemisphere-expansion.md)

Expand from US/Canada to the entire Western Hemisphere. New country and subdivision models, updated range fields, revised search and filtering.

### [Maps](.mull/matters/4143-maps-rework.md)

The current US/Canada mapping approach doesn't scale to hemisphere coverage. This is the highest-risk work stream and needs prototyping before committing to an approach.

### Taxonomy & Data at Scale

- **[Taxonomy management tools](.mull/matters/5b3d-taxonomy-management-tools.md)** — Split, merge, and move taxa efficiently at hemisphere scale.
- **[DNA barcode / taxonomic data integration](.mull/matters/1374-dna-barcode-taxonomic-data-integration.md)** — DNA data accessibility has exploded since the site launched. Increasingly critical for taxonomy resolution, especially as we grow geographically.
- **[iNaturalist integration](.mull/matters/8ba0-inaturalist-integration.md)** — Photo import with proper attribution, observation-informed range and abundance data, taxonomic change tracking. Reduces admin burden while expanding coverage.

### Admin & Community Growth

- **[Permissions and roles refinement](.mull/matters/ec68-permissions-and-roles-refinement.md)** — More granular than the current visitor/admin/superadmin model. Needed as the admin base grows with hemisphere expansion.
- **[Admin workflow improvements](.mull/matters/9005-admin-workflow-improvements.md)** — Informed by Phase 1 onboarding research and the practical needs of hemisphere-scale data entry. iNat integration is a key part of this: pointing at an observation and pulling photos with attribution saves significant manual work.
- **[Host plant data sourcing](.mull/matters/7932-host-plant-data-sourcing-for-central-south-america.md)** — Central and South American host plant data sources are limited. Research needed to identify viable data pipelines.

## Phase 3: Gall Associates — Parasites, Inquilines & Beyond

*Vertical depth — this is the biggest item on the roadmap.*

The biological community around galls is a huge and actively researched area. Parasites, inquilines, and hyperparasites associated with galls represent a major expansion of what the site covers.

### [Data Model Design](.mull/matters/bf97-parasites-and-inquilines-expansion.md)

New association model linking galls to their biological associates. This is deep domain modeling work that requires careful design — the data relationships are complex and the research landscape is evolving.

### [Leaf Miners](.mull/matters/e29c-leaf-miners-assessment.md)

Assess whether leaf miners fit the same expansion pattern or need different treatment.

### [Oaks Site Integration](.mull/matters/5d49-oaks-site-integration.md)

Cross-link between Gallformers and the Oaks site (also Elixir, also has an API). At minimum: host pages on Gallformers link to the Oaks site and vice versa.

### Additional Data Model Work

- [Generation field for gall traits](.mull/matters/0a58-generation-field-for-gall-traits.md) — Track generation (e.g., sexual vs asexual) as a gall trait

### Implications

This phase will likely drive further admin tooling needs, additional taxonomy management capabilities, and potentially new identification approaches.

## Sequencing Logic

The three main phases have a natural dependency:

1. **Phase 1** makes the platform reliable and the admin team bigger
2. **Phase 2** proves we can scale geographically with that team
3. **Phase 3** is the deepest domain expansion and benefits from everything learned in Phases 1 and 2

Foundation work is always mixed in with visible features. The goal is never pure infrastructure or pure features — it's always both, with infrastructure slightly ahead of the features that need it.

The scarcest resource is domain expertise and architectural judgment. Work that requires deep knowledge of gall biology and data modeling (Phases 2-3) takes priority during periods of high availability. Work that others can drive (documentation, interoperability exports, UI refinements) can happen anytime.
